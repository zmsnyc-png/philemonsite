PHILEMON

Philemon takes its name from a winged figure who appeared to Carl Jung in his visions and active imagination. Jung described Philemon as a presence who seemed to know more than he did, a companion that revealed understanding through reflection, imagination, and dialogue. A space that carries that figure’s meaning into physical form through shared rituals that expand the mind, allowing a shifted state to emerge.
